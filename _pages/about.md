I am Dr. James E. Andrews and am a Professor at the School of Information, University of South Florida, where I am the William W. and Judith A. Gaunt Endowed Professor in Library and Information Science. My research intersects information science and health informatics, and I collaborate with a range of faculty and other partners. Recently, we have been developing a student health informatics research lab with students from different programs across USF, and are continually seeking new collaborators (especially faculty mentors) to develop new research projects.

I received a Doctor of Philosophy in Information Science from the University of Missouri – Columbia, where he also earned a Master’s in Library and Information Science. My doctoral work was funded by the National Institutes of Health (NIH), National Library of Medicine (NLM) as a Predoctoral Medical Informatics Fellow. My research focuses on topics in health information behaviors and communication, standards and controlled terminologies, and various topics in library and information science. I've been an investigator on several federally-funded grants (NIH, DoD, AHRQ, CDC), and I am co-editor of the first book on Clinical Research Informatics (Springer-Verlag), which is now in its third edition. More information can be found on my CV and in various pages of this site.  

I encourage anyone to reach out who may have some shared interests or otherwise have ideas for collaborating.
